About Me

Midwestern Roots

I was born and raised in the suburbs of Indianapolis, Indiana. I attended Fishers High School where I excelled in academics. My favorite subjects were mathematics, Spanish, and United States government. I played the saxophone in the high school band and was also a member of the school debate team. I graduated near the top of my class all four years and was a National Merit Scholar.

Professional Background

After graduating high school I attended Texas A&M University and graduated with a bachelors in mechanical engineering, with minors in computer science and Spanish. During my senior year, I interned at an automation company located in Dallas, where I first gained exposure to working on a software team. I continued my education at Texas A&M and graduated with a masters in mechanical engineering with a specialization in robotics in December of 2020. During my time as a graduate student, I gained both hands-on and theoretical experience with robotics and collaborated with industry and other academic institutions as part of my research.
